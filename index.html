Merkles Family Memories

Once upon a time, in a quiet little forest nestled between mountains, there lived a peculiar bird named Frank. Frank wasn't your average bird. He wore a tiny pair of round glasses and always carried a leather-bound notebook under his wing.

Frank had two siblings: Roy, who was very grumpy, and Dave, who always seemed confused. But together, they made up the Merkle family—a bird family unlike any other.

Every morning, Frank would wake up early to record his dreams, which were often full of adventure and bug-chasing antics. He believed bugs were everywhere—bug conspiracies, bug spies, and even bug hackers.

One day, Frank spotted a curious little raccoon rummaging through a data chip he found behind a tree. “Aha!” Frank exclaimed. “A data thief!”

He followed the raccoon only to discover an underground lair full of electronic bugs shaped like goats, cats, mice, and more. They weren’t real animals—they were adorable little critters designed to spy, steal, or snoop.

Frank made it his mission to document each one of them in his notebook, giving them quirky names like Null Bug, Raccoon Hacker, and SniffleMouse. He even drew pictures of each for his top-secret files.

In the end, Frank and his birdy brothers saved the forest from the Bugpocalypse (barely), and every creature lived more cautiously ever after.

This story is based on real drawings, real laughs, and real imagination. The bugs are real—just not in the way you think.
